World War 1

Overview

World War 1, also known as the Great War, was a global conflict that lasted from 1914 to 1918. It involved most of the world's great powers, organized into two opposing alliances: the Allies and the Central Powers.

Causes

The main causes of World War 1 included militarism, alliances, imperialism, and nationalism. The assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand of Austria-Hungary in 1914 served as a trigger for the war.

Major Events

  • Assassination of Archduke Franz Ferdinand
  • Outbreak of war in Europe
  • Trench warfare on the Western Front
  • Battles of Verdun and the Somme
  • Entry of the United States into the war
  • Russian Revolution
  • Armistice of 11 November 1918

Consequences

World War 1 had far-reaching consequences, including the collapse of empires, the redrawing of national boundaries, the Treaty of Versailles, and the League of Nations. It also set the stage for World War 2.
